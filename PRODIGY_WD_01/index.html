    • Mountains

    In the cradle of ancient earth they rise, Majestic peaks that touch the skies. Silent sentinels of nature's art, Where whispers of wind etch stories in every part. Their rugged faces, weathered and grand, Hold secrets of ages, by time's gentle hand. Cloaked in veils of mist and morning dew, They paint a canvas of ever-changing hue. In their shadowed valleys, life finds a way, Flourishing beneath the sun's warm ray. From icy glaciers to flowered meadows, A symphony of life in their embrace grows. Oh, mountains, your beauty knows no bound, In your presence, serenity is found. With peaks that pierce the boundless sky, You lift our spirits, you make us sigh. So stand proud, O mountains, timeless and tall, Guardians of nature, majestic in all. For in your silent splendor, we find peace, A beauty that forever will never cease.
